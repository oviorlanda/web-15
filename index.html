About Opticom Laboratory

Optical Communication System Laboratory (Opticomm) is one of the transmission laboratories at Telkom University which is engaged in optical communication technology. The Opticomm Laboratory was inaugurated in 1999, and has main activity in terms of organizing practicum for the Optical Communication System course for bachelor students of Telecommunication Engineering at Telkom University.

In addition to practical activities, we also conduct research that focuses on optical wireless communication, such as: Visible Light Communication, Vehicle to Vehicle Communication, Underwater Visible Light Communication, Hybrid Radio Frequency-Visible Light Communication, Free Space Optic, Underground Mining Visible Light Communication, and many more.

Bina Desa

The Village Development Program is an initiative aimed at improving the quality of life in rural areas through the empowerment of local resources and the development of various sectors, such as education, healthcare, economy, and infrastructure. This program gives special attention to remote areas that may have had limited access to services and development.

Through collaboration between the government, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and the local community, the Village Development Program strives to build the capacity of local residents in managing natural resources and enhancing economic, social, and cultural independence.

The main focus of this program is sustainable development, where every effort is made with consideration for environmental sustainability and the long-term self-sufficiency of the village. Activities carried out include skills training for the community, providing better educational and healthcare facilities, and developing infrastructure that facilitates access and mobility for residents.

The program also encourages active community participation in the planning and implementation processes to ensure that the solutions taken truly meet the needs and potentials of the village. Through the Village Development Program, it is hoped that more advanced, independent, and prosperous villages will emerge, capable of overcoming the challenges faced by rural communities in adapting to changing times.
